Apart from my [academic research](https://www.semanticscholar.org/author/Grayson-Badgley/9659424), I've spent the last several years writing about the science and political economy of global carbon market. 
That has included:

- investigating the empty promises of [using the blockchain to revolutionize carbon markets](https://carbonplan.org/research/crypto-toucan-explainer); 
- documenting the unwillingness of the market to [properly insure](https://slate.com/technology/2024/02/carbon-offsets-califo
rnia-fire-neutral-shipping-climate-change.html) [forest-backed offset projects](https://doi.org/10.1111/gcb.17599) that inevitably burn;
- highlighting [structual problems](https://carbonplan.org/research/kariba-excess-credits) that incentivize market participants to turn a blind eye to the existence of low-quality offsets.

Much of this work has involved working with journalists at outlets like [ProPublica](https://www.propublica.org/article/the-climate-solution-actually-adding-millions-of-tons-of-co2-into-the-atmosphere), [National Geographic](https://www.nationalgeographic.com/environment/article/forests-as-carbon-offsets-climate-change-has-other-plans), and the [LA Times](https://www.latimes.com/politics/story/2021-09-08/what-is-the-california-climate-credit-does-it-cut-pollution) to explore the inner-workings of the market.
